Global Temperatures

This graph shows global, hemisphere & lattitude-specific temperatures from 1880 to 2014. The Y-axis shows the temperature in degrees celsius while the X-axis shows the current year.

The global trend seems to be only slightly increasing from 1880-1940, followed by a peak during 1944 and then a more rapid increase from 1955 and on.

The northern hemisphere rises above the southern hemisphere by approximately 20 degrees Celsius after 1998.

Enabling the Lattitude Zone lines & disabling the other lines allows us to see that the 24N-90N section of the Northern Hemisphere is a much warmer than the rest of the earth after 1998, with temperatures 40-60 degrees higher than 90S-24S and 20-40 degrees higher than 24S-24N.
